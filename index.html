Overview: 7 Essential Objectives of Puerperal Nursing

Based on Pillitteri, the puerperium (first 6 weeks after childbirth) requires focused nursing care to promote physical recovery, prevent complications, and support family adaptation. The 7 essential objectives are:

1️⃣ Prevent Hemorrhage
Maintain uterine tone via fundal massage and oxytocics; monitor lochia.
2️⃣ Promote Bladder/Bowel Function
Prevent urinary retention and constipation; encourage fluids/fiber.
3️⃣ Prevent Infection
Perineal care, hand hygiene, monitor vital signs and lochia odor.
4️⃣ Promote Comfort & Pain Relief
Manage afterpains, perineal discomfort, breast engorgement.
5️⃣ Support Breastfeeding
Latch assistance, milk production, mastitis prevention.
6️⃣ Emotional & Psychological Support
Identify baby blues, PPD, psychosis; provide reassurance and referral.
7️⃣ Discharge Teaching & Family Integration
Newborn care, postpartum warning signs, contraception, follow‑up.
Pillitteri emphasis: These objectives are interrelated; achieving them ensures optimal maternal–infant outcomes.

1. Prevent Hemorrhage (Uterine Tone)

Goal: Maintain uterine contraction and prevent excessive blood loss.

  • Fundal assessment: Check position, firmness (should be midline and firm like a grapefruit). If boggy → massage gently until firm.
  • Lochia monitoring: Rubra (1–3 days), Serosa (4–10 days), Alba (11 days–6 weeks). Abnormal: >1 pad/hour, large clots, foul odor.
  • Vital signs: Tachycardia and hypotension suggest hypovolemia.
  • Bladder care: Empty bladder every 2–4 hours; distension prevents contraction.
  • Medications: Oxytocin (Pitocin), methylergonovine (Methergine), carboprost (Hemabate), misoprostol.
⚠️ Warning signs: Heavy bleeding, uterine atony, large clots, dizziness → notify provider immediately.

2. Promote Bladder & Bowel Function

  • Bladder: Void within 6–8 hours after delivery. Assess for distension (palpable bladder above fundus). Diuresis occurs 2–5 days postpartum.
  • Interventions: Encourage fluids, assist to bathroom, run water, use warm perineal irrigation. Catheterize if unable to void.
  • Bowel: First bowel movement usually 2–3 days postpartum. Prevent constipation: high‑fiber diet, stool softeners (docusate), hydration, ambulation.
  • Episiotomy care: Stool softeners reduce pain. Avoid straining and enemas.
Pillitteri note: Full bladder can displace the fundus and cause atony → hemorrhage. Frequent voiding is essential.

3. Prevent Infection (Perineal & Uterine)

  • Perineal care: Wipe front to back, change perineal pads every 2–4 hours, use peri‑bottle with warm water after voiding.
  • Endometritis prevention: Monitor lochia for foul odor, uterine tenderness, fever >100.4°F (38°C).
  • Hand hygiene: Strict handwashing before and after perineal care.
  • Wound care: Episiotomy or C‑section incision – assess for redness, edema, ecchymosis, discharge, approximation (REEDA).
🩺 Fever workup: Rule out endometritis, UTI, mastitis, wound infection, thrombophlebitis.

4. Promote Comfort & Pain Relief

  • Afterpains: More common in multiparas and with breastfeeding. Mild analgesics (ibuprofen), positioning, relaxation techniques.
  • Perineal discomfort: Ice packs (first 24 hours), sitz baths (after 24 hours), topical anesthetics (witch hazel pads), NSAIDs.
  • Breast engorgement: Frequent feeding, warm compresses before feeding, cold packs after feeding, cabbage leaves (controversial but traditional).
  • Episiotomy/C‑section pain: Oral analgesics (acetaminophen, ibuprofen); for C‑section: early ambulation, incentive spirometry.

5. Support Breastfeeding

  • Initiation: First feeding within 1 hour after birth (skin‑to‑skin).
  • Latch assessment: Wide mouth, flanged lips, areola visible above, pain‑free nursing.
  • Frequency: 8–12 feedings in 24 hours, on demand (every 2–3 hours).
  • Signs of adequate intake: 6–8 wet diapers/day, 3–4 stools/day (after day 3), steady weight gain, contentment after feeds.
  • Common problems: Sore nipples (improve latch), engorgement (feed frequently), mastitis (continue feeding, antibiotics).
  • Contraindications: HIV, active untreated TB, maternal chemotherapy, galactosemia in infant.
Pillitteri: Support from nurses significantly increases breastfeeding duration and success.

6. Emotional & Psychological Support

  • Assess mood: Use Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS).
  • Baby blues: Reassurance, rest, support; resolves in 2 weeks.
  • PPD: Persistent sadness, anhedonia, guilt → refer for counseling/medication.
  • Postpartum psychosis: Delusions, hallucinations, risk of infanticide → immediate psychiatric hospitalization.
  • Partner involvement: Include partner in education and care; assess for paternal depression.
🚨 Emergency signs: Suicidal/homicidal ideation, command hallucinations, disorganized behavior → 1:1 observation, notify psychiatry.

7. Discharge Teaching & Family Integration

  • Postpartum warning signs (call provider): Fever >100.4°F, foul lochia, severe headache, vision changes, chest pain, calf pain, bleeding >1 pad/hour.
  • Perineal care at home: Continue peri‑bottle, sitz baths, pad changes q4h, avoid tampons, douching, intercourse (6 weeks).
  • Contraception: Ovulation can occur as early as 3 weeks (non‑lactating) or 6 months (lactating). Discuss LARC, condoms, mini‑pill.
  • Newborn care: Safe sleep (back to sleep), feeding cues, cord care, follow‑up appointments.
  • Family integration: Support sibling adjustment, encourage partner involvement, identify community resources.
Follow‑up: Postpartum visit at 2–3 weeks (high‑risk) or 6 weeks (routine).

Mnemonics & Summary Table

📌 "BUBBLE-HE" – Postpartum Assessment
Breasts, Uterus, Bladder, Bowels, Lochia, Episiotomy, Homan's sign (for DVT), Emotions.
🩸 "Tone, Tissue, Trauma, Thrombin" (4 T's of PPH)
Prevention: fundal massage, oxytocin, inspect placenta, avoid lacerations, correct coagulopathy.
🚨 REEDA – Perineal assessment
Redness, Edema, Ecchymosis, Discharge, Approximation.
🍼 Breastfeeding: "C.H.O.S.E."
C – Comfortable positioning, H – Head alignment, O – Open mouth, S – Suckling, E – Effective swallowing.
📋 Discharge Mnemonic "P.A.S.S."
P – Postpartum warning signs, A – Activity (rest, avoid heavy lifting), S – Sexual activity (wait 6 weeks), S – Support resources.
🎯 7 Objectives in sequence
Bleeding → Bladder → Infection → Comfort → Breastfeeding → Emotions → Discharge.
